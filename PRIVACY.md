# Privacy Policy for VigilantEar 👂🛰️

**Effective Date:** May 12, 2026

## Introduction

VigilantEar ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

## Information We Collect

VigilantEar processes data **entirely on your device**. We do **not** collect or transmit any personal identifiable information (PII) data anywhere.

The app accesses (with your permission) the following information **locally**:

- **Microphone audio** – Used in real-time to detect sounds (sirens, vehicles, etc.).
- **Location data** – Used to display the direction and estimated distance of detected sounds on the map.
- **Device orientation and motion** – Used to improve directional accuracy.

Competitor apps often send data to the cloud and share it for profit. VigilantEar does the opposite. We eliminate all of that.

We *do* keep a single, one-time-only log entry for our own internal purposes (mostly so we feel good that our hard work is being used). Here's a sample of exactly what's in that log entry: appVersion: "1.0.56", installedAt: "May 11, 2026 at 9:09:56.004 PM UTC-7", osVersion: "26.4.2", and systemName: "iOS".  As you can see, there is no PII or device information in that log.

## How We Use Your Information

All processing happens **on-device**. This information is used by the app solely to provide real-time acoustic awareness and directional guidance.

We do **not**:

- Store audio sound streams or recordings
- Send any PII data to the cloud or to internet-connected servers
- Use analytics or tracking tools

## Data Sharing

We do not share any personally identifiable information (PII) or device information with third parties because we do not collect or transmit any.

## On-Device Processing

VigilantEar is designed with privacy in mind. All sound detection, location processing, and map rendering happens locally on your phone. No PII leaves your device.

We *do* log minimal application exceptions to the cloud without any PII data or sound streams. For example, when a new sound type is detected that the ML chip supports but isn't optimized within our app yet. That log consists of the unrecognized sound type and a date: soundType: "cow_moo", timestamp: "May 11, 2026 at 9:09:56.004 PM UTC-7". That is obviously not an emergency or alertable sound type so we would mark it as excluded - yet still show it on the "live" sound ML list on the right-hand side of the screen for awareness. And, translate it from English ("牛哞" or "niú mōu" in Chinese, in this nonsensical example).

## Your Rights

Since we do not collect or store your personal data, there is no need to request deletion or access. You remain in full control of your data at all times.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Although, there isn't much *less* we can do and we'll never change that. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Effective Date" at the top of this page.

You control everything. You can turn off the microphone for background phone alerts (in our settings panel) or revoke any device permissions via the OS. We follow Apple’s strict guidelines and will update this policy if anything changes.

❤️ VigilantEar is built with love and respect for the Deaf and hard-of-hearing community. Your trust matters to us.
